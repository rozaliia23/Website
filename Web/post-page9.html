Used to

Past habits

We use used to when we refer to things in the past which are no longer true.

It can refer to repeated actions or to a state or situation:

He used to play football for the local team, but he’s too old now.
That white house over there used to belong to my family. (It belonged to my family in the past, but not any more.)

Warning: In statements, the form used to does not change. We do not use the verb be before it. It always refers to past time:

We used to go to the seaside every summer when I was a kid.

Not: We are used to go … or We use to go … or We were used to go …
